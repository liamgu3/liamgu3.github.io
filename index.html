Sky-Caper

Sky-Caper is a stealth platformer developed by myself and two teammates, Julien Chaulot and Sean Foley, over the course of an entire summer. My role in this project was primarily programming, with a focus on the AI programming. I also collaborated on the game's design, level design, and publishing. A Melody from East Berlin

A Melody from East Berlin is branching narrative rpg about attempting to escape East Berlin by crossing the Berlin Wall. This game was developed in 9 days for the Historically Accurate Game Jam 3, in which it got 7th place out of 89 submissions. I was the sole programmer on the project, working with an artist and musician to create this game. This game was developed in Unity.

Color Dodge

Color Dodge is a fast paced arcade game I developed myself in 72 hours for Mini Jam 62: Colors. I created everything for this game myself except for the background art, including the programming, design, music, sound effects, and UI. This game was developed in Unity.

Urban Decay

Urban Decay is a real time dungeon crawler with 3d graphics developed by myself and 4 teammates over 5 weeks. My primary role on this project was developing the level generator that allowed for rapid iteration on our levels and the potential for creating more levels in the future. I also created the menu structure and implemented all the audio. This game was developed in Unity.

Murder Case

Murder Case is a narrative driven walking simulator developed by myself and four of my classmates. My role in this project was as a 3D Modeler, modeling the dining room furniture, the bathroom, and wall paintings. This game was developed in Unity and the models were created in Maya.

Running Routes

Running Routes is a tool that utilizes the Strava API, MapBox API, and the OpenWeather API to tell the user the closest running routes of a specified length and what clothing they should wear for the current weather. It was created using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript and developed solely by me.

The Hill Cumorah Legacy Project

The Hill Cumorah Legacy Project was a public history project to digitally preserve the memory of the Hill Cumorah Pageant, a pageant put on by the LDS Church in Palmyra, New York for over 80 years. I interviewed pageant participants and digitally archived artifacts and documents relating to the pageant, all of which was uploaded to Omeka for preservation.

Consuming Empires: British Arctic Exploration and The Franklin Expedition

This essay examines the roles science, technology, and imperialistic fervor played in the ill-fated Sir Franklin Expedition to find the Northwest Passage in 1845. This essay recieved the Kearse Student Writing Award given by the RIT College of Liberal Arts for excellent student writing.

The Lowell Six

This research essay investigates six deaf graduates of the American School for the Deaf who lived and worked together in Lowell, Massachusetts at the dawn of the American Industrial Revolution. The research is compiled entirely from first-hand research of archival sources. Research on this project is ongoing.

The Sling: A Brief History and Experimental Recreation

This essay documents an experimental archaelogy project to recreate an ancient American sling found in Peru. The project involved researching the materials and methods used to create the sling and attempting to replicate this process for educational purposes.
